Our Story

At First Sight March 2012

Our story begins on a seemingly exciting but wonted Friday night in San Francisco. But with Stephanie and Andrew both headed to a mutual friend (Alice)'s birthday party at The Grand, it turns out that what fate had in store was something else entirely. Running into another mutual friend (Rita) in rather felicitous circumstances, Andrew and Stephanie were finally introduced. Needless to say, it was love at first sight. Kind of. OK, not really. But the short and sobriety-free introduction did lead to a first date, which is really all that matters.

The First Date March 2012

A few days after meeting, Stephanie and Andrew made plans to head to a beer garden the next weekend. But when the time came, it was raining and the rendezvous moved indoors to La Folie. Between the last minute changes and apparent poor communication, there was a bit of confusion. Andrew wasn't sure it was actually a date, and Stephanie wasn't sure she wanted to go. As a result, both made plans with friends for later in the night and expected the date to be short. Despite all of that, as well as Stephanie's original shyness, they actually hit it off quite well, and Andrew went with Stephanie to meet up with her friends. After a fun night of dancing, they had their first kiss as Andrew dropped Stephanie off at home.

The Proposal September 2016

Four and a half years after meeting, Stephanie and Andrew ventured to Europe on a bit of a while-were-still-young adventure. But after partying in Ibiza and prior to enjoying a few drinks at Oktoberfest, they settled into a few romantic days on the French Riviera. On their final night, Andrew brought Stephanie to what she thought would be only a beautiful dinner pearched above the sea in the mountain town of Eze. But before dinner, they wandered up to a small cactus garden atop the village. It was here, just before sunset, that Andrew began to recite a poem he had written. Dropping to one knee, and with a photographer hidden away to capture the moment, he read the final line: The only thing left in chapter one / Is to ask "will you marry me?"
