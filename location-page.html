Sonoma, CA

Getting Around

Because most guests are local, those travelling have myriad preferences, and lodging is sparse and can be expensive in Sonoma, we think it makes the most sense to provide transportation to and from San Francisco. Pickup will be 2:30pm at the Westfield Mall's Mission St entrance, returning at the conclusion of the reception.

For those that do choose to stay in beautiful Sonoma, we strongly recommend renting a car. There will be ample parking at the wedding venue, taxis and Uber/Lyft can be hard to come by in Sonoma, and public transportation is virtually non-existant. If you need help arranging transportation to or from the wedding, let us know and we'll be happy to assist.

Where to Stay

If choosing to stay in Sonoma, the best options are likely to be found on AirBnB or HomeAway. Hotels in May can be expensive, fill up quickly, and often require a two night minimum. That said, The Lodge and The Best Western in downtown Sonoma are good central hotels. The Sonoma Mission Inn is fantastic for those looking for something a bit more upscale and The Sonoma Creek Inn across the street is a good less expensive option. About 15 minutes away, Petaluma often has more options as well.

If choosing to stay in San Francisco, we recommend the Marriott Marquis. It's a good hotel and a good location, and we'll try to provide transportation from as close by as possible.

What to Do

The most obvious thing to do in Sonoma is wine tasting! Unlike in Napa Valley across the way, many wineries in Sonoma allow you to bring your own food and provide space for a picnic. While most of the vineyards in the area have great wines, we love the intimate tucked away vibe and lush lavendar gardens at Matanzas Creek, as well as the sweeping panoramic views at Viansa. Bartholomew Park also offers some great outdoors space and hiking trails to go with their wines. With so many world class wineries in the area, it's really hard to go wrong here.

If you're looking for something else to do, you can always check out the famous Sonoma Mission, go for a drive on the world-renowned Sonoma Raceway, or wander around the main square, littered with fantastic local shops and restaurants! Cline Cellars, another great winery, also has a lovely mission museum.

San Francisco

If you choose to spend time in San Francisco, opportunities are endless. Some of our favorite things to do are hike to Lands End (amazing views of the Golden Gate bridge), wander around and get Dim Sum in Chinatown, go to the market at the Ferry Building, (window) shop around Union Square, and grab a drink at the Tipsy Pig. Alcatraz is newly renovated and would be an amazing addition to any trip, though advance reservations are usually required.

While we'll have quite the party Saturday night, Sunday morning is the infamous Bay To Breakers 12k race that turns into a bit of a debaucherous costume party by 8AM and is a great and unique San Francisco experience.
